# NYC-Motor-Vehicle-Collisions

## Project Overview

This project analyzes NYC motor vehicle collision data to identify accident patterns and major contributing factors.


## Objectives

- Analyze the monthly trend of traffic collisions in New York City between 2021 and 2023.
- Identify the days and hours with the highest number of reported traffic collisions.
- Determine the streets with the highest number of reported traffic accidents.
- Identify the most common contributing factors responsible for traffic collisions.
- Determine the vehicle types most frequently involved in traffic collisions.
- Analyze the contributing factors associated with fatal accidents.
- Provide data-driven recommendations to support road safety improvements and informed decision-making by relevant stakeholders.


## Tools Used

  ## Insights

1. March recorded the highest number of traffic collisions, making it the peak month for reported accidents during the 2021–2023 period.

2. The highest number of collisions occurred at 4:00 PM, with accident volumes remaining consistently high between 3:00 PM and 6:00 PM, indicating that the evening rush hour is the highest-risk period.

3. Fridays recorded the highest number of traffic collisions, making it the most accident-prone day of the week.

4. Belt Parkway accounted for approximately 1.56% of all reported collisions, making it the street with the highest number of reported accidents in the dataset.

5. Driver Inattention/Distraction was the leading contributing factor to traffic collisions, highlighting distracted driving as the most common cause of crashes.

6. Passenger vehicles were the most frequently involved vehicle type, indicating that they account for the largest share of reported collisions.

7. Analysis of fatal accidents identified a key contributing factor which is Driver inattentiveness that should be prioritized in road safety interventions.

   ## Recommendations

1. Increase traffic enforcement during March, as it recorded the highest number of collisions. Additional road safety campaigns and traffic management strategies should be implemented before and during this period.

2. Deploy more traffic officers and emergency response teams between 3:00 PM and 6:00 PM, particularly around 4:00 PM, when collision rates are at their highest. Increased monitoring during these peak hours can help reduce accidents and improve response times.

3. Strengthen traffic management on Fridays, as this is the day with the highest number of reported collisions. This could include increased patrols, traffic control measures, and public awareness initiatives.

4. Prioritize safety improvements on Belt Parkway, including road safety audits, improved signage, better lighting where necessary, and enhanced enforcement to reduce collisions on this high-risk roadway.

5. Implement stronger measures to reduce distracted driving, since Driver Inattention/Distraction is the leading contributing factor. This should include stricter enforcement, public education campaigns, and initiatives promoting responsible driving behavior.

6. Develop targeted road safety programs for passenger vehicle drivers, as passenger vehicles are involved in the largest proportion of reported collisions. Driver education and awareness campaigns should focus on this group.

7. Continue monitoring and investigating fatal collisions to identify their primary causes and implement targeted interventions aimed at reducing traffic-related fatalities.
